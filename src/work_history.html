Professional History and Relevant Skills

Retail Supervisor - Melbourne Zoo

Main responsibilities included:

  • Staff Management
  • Staff Development and Training
  • Sales and KPI Management

Successfully lead the team in record sales years totalling over $5million, and in inaugural retail campaigns such as Totes for Wildlife.
Communication and driving sales were key here, a large team of 30 casuals and 4 part time staff were under my management, as well as a wider team of 75 in the Visitor Services team to which I was a leader.
Key to the success of the team was communicating a large variety of product, and conservation campaigns to a wide variety of individual visitors.
As well as in store, I worked to create a document to track units of uniform for over 300 staff at Melbourne Zoo through excel. Ended up using a bunch of macros and logic. Reignited my love for code!
After six years at the zoo I found that the best approach was a visitor centric, relying on genuine and tailored interactions. It was my job to pass this on to my entire team of casuals!

Store Manager and Area Visual Merchandiser

Main responsibilities included:

  • Visual Merchandising
  • Personal Styling
  • Sales

Jobshared a position as Store Manager for the DFO Southwharf concept store, as well as Visual Merchandising for the Brooksfield section in 10 Myer locations across the Melbourne Metro area.
Visual Merchandising was the main focus, as the areas for Brooksfield were managed most of the time by Myer staff, we needed to ensure a uniform style across locations.
My job was to ensure all of our items were styled correctly, and grouped according to seasonal colour lines and fits.
The joy in creating outfits, and dressing customers for formal events was awesome! I learned a lot about how to ask the right questions to find out exactly what customers wanted.
Of course more communication was involved, as I often had to dress and work to the concensus of a large party of groomsmen for weddings and similar events.
